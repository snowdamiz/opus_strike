# Phase 1: Map Foundation - Context

**Gathered:** 2026-01-22
**Status:** Ready for planning

<domain>
## Phase Boundary

Establish the playable arena — remove the old imported map, create an asymmetrical ground layout with distinct Team A and Team B areas, and define three routes connecting the two sides. This phase creates the floor geometry and basic structure. Cover elements, spawns, flags, and decoration are separate phases.

</domain>

<decisions>
## Implementation Decisions

### Visual Style
- Sci-fi/futuristic aesthetic throughout
- Dark color scheme (grays/blacks) with glowing colored accents (cyan, magenta)
- Heavy sci-fi elements: floating platforms, energy barriers, lots of glow effects
- Team differentiation: Team A has red/orange accents + warmer lighting, Team B has blue/cyan accents + cooler lighting

### Layout Shape
- Fully asymmetric layout — each team side has fundamentally different design
- Terrain contrast: one side has platforms/tech structures, other has natural terrain/caves
- Rectangular arena shape overall (classic elongated rectangle, teams on opposite ends)
- Tall physical walls as map boundary (high enough that players cannot pass)

### Route Character
- Three routes differentiated by engagement distance:
  - Close-quarters route: tunnels/corridors with tight turns and limited escape
  - Medium-range route: standard mixed terrain
  - Long-range route: open elevated platform with clear sightlines (exposed position)
- Routes are interconnected mid-map — passages between routes allow flanking

### Ground Features
- Mostly flat surfaces with distinct elevation changes via stairs and platforms
- Void edges AND hazard zones present
- Hazards at strategic mid-map locations (not just perimeter) for risk/reward positioning
- No water/liquid features — clean solid surfaces

### Claude's Discretion
- Exact platform heights and stair placement
- Specific glow colors and intensity
- Tunnel/corridor dimensions
- How routes interconnect (number and placement of cross-passages)
- Precise hazard zone placement and size

</decisions>
